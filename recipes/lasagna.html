Lasagna

Lasagna is a layered Italian pasta dish known for its rich and comforting flavors. It features sheets of pasta stacked with layers of savory fillings, which typically include a tomato-based meat sauce, creamy ricotta or béchamel, and gooey melted cheese.

Baked to golden perfection, lasagna offers a hearty and satisfying texture, with the layers combining to create a harmonious blend of flavors. It’s a beloved dish, often associated with family meals and special occasions, showcasing the essence of Italian comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (200 g) graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup (115 g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 24 oz (680 g) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup (200 g) granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) sour cream
  • 1/4 cup (60 ml) heavy cream

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C) and grease a 9-inch (23 cm) springform pan.
  2. In a bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter until evenly combined. Press the mixture into the bottom of the pan to form the crust.
  3. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then set aside to cool.
  4. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar until smooth and creamy.
  5. Add the vanilla extract and eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  6. Mix in the sour cream and heavy cream until fully incorporated.
  7. Pour the filling over the prepared crust and smooth the top with a spatula.
  8. Bake the cheesecake for 50-60 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  9. Turn off the oven, crack the door open slightly, and let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour.
  10. Refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight before serving.
