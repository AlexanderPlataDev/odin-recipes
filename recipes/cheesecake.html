Cheesecake

Cheesecake is a creamy and indulgent dessert made with a rich mixture of cream cheese, sugar, and eggs, typically baked on a crumbly crust made from crushed cookies or graham crackers. Its smooth, velvety texture and balance of sweetness make it a versatile treat that can be served plain or topped with a variety of flavors, such as fresh fruits, chocolate, or caramel.

This classic dessert is cherished worldwide for its luxurious taste and adaptability, with countless variations ranging from no-bake recipes to flavored infusions like lemon, pumpkin, or vanilla. It’s a centerpiece dessert perfect for celebrations or casual indulgence.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (200 g) graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup (115 g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup (200 g) granulated sugar
  • 24 oz (680 g) cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) sour cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • Pie filling (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C) and grease a 9-inch (23 cm) springform pan.
  2. Combine the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter, then press the mixture into the bottom of the pan to form the crust. Bake for 10 minutes and let it cool.
  3. Prepare the filling by beating the cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add the vanilla, eggs (one at a time), sour cream, and heavy cream, mixing until fully combined.
  4. Pour the filling over the cooled crust and smooth the top.
  5. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  6. Run a knife around the edges to loosen the sides and let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour with the door slightly open.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight before removing from the pan and serving. Add optional pie filling if desired.
